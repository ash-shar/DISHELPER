What does the system do?

Microblogging sites like Twitter have become important sources of real-time information over the past decade especially during emergency scenarios. During a disaster, various types of information are posted by users in huge volume and at rapid rates, which include situational information (status updates like number of casualties, current situation in regions affected by disaster etc.), sentiment (e.g., sympathy for those affected by the disaster) and personal opinions (e.g., on the adequacy of relief operations). To effectively utilize the microblogging sites as an aid to the relief operations during disaster events, the following two tasks are essential:
  1. Extracting situational information from among the large amounts of sentiment and opinion. Classification of posts into Situational and Non-Situational catetgories is required.
  2. Summarizing situational information to help decision-making processes when time is critical.
DisHelper efficiently caters both of these tasks by making use of disaster-specific typicalities.
