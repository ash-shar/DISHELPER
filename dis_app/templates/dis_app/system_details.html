Type of Tweets during disasters

During disasters, large number of tweets are posted within a short timespan. These tweets are broadly classified into two categories:
  1. Situational Tweets (status updates like number of casualties, current situation in regions affected by disaster etc.): Ex. state police are responding to a report of a shooting at an elementary school in newtown
  2. Non-situational Tweets (Sentiment/Opinion, Event analysis, Charities): Ex. thoughts/prayers for everyone in the path of #typhoon hope lessons from #haiyan will save lives
A significant number of tweets contain mixture of situational and non-situational tweets, Ex. ayyo! not again! :( Blasts in Hyderabad, 7 Killed: TV REPORTS . Hence, we first separate out different parts and process them separately.

Broad Framework of our System

Classification of Tweets Into Situational and Non-Situational Categories

We observe that people generally follow some particular trend in case of posting non-situational tweets. In this work, we try to capture those signals instead of traditional vocabularies used in a particular scenario. This observation helps us to develop a classifier which is able to classify tweets into situational and non-situational categories independent of the vocabulary used during a particular disaster event. In summary, the trained classifer model can be efficiently used for a future disaster event.

Generating Summary of a Disaster Event

Some specific words (Content Words) play a key role during disaster scenario.
  • Numerals (report information about helplines, number of victims)
  • Nouns (important context words like people, hospital etc.)
  • Locations
  • Main verbs (captures event phrases like "killed", "injured" etc.)
These content words grow slowly during disaster events compared to other general events.

We summarize the situational tweet stream by optimizing the coverage of important content words in the summary, using an Integer Linear Programming (ILP) framework.
